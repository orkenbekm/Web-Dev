Cats

Domestic cats (Felis catus) are small, carnivorous mammals in the Felidae family, domesticated for nearly 10,000 years, with a population of about 600 million worldwide. They are known for being independent, territorial, and skillful hunters that sleep 12–18 hours daily. Popular as pets, they require, or provide companionship, and communicate through vocalizations (purrs, meows) and, scent-marking.
